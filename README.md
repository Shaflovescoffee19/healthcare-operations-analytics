# Healthcare Operations Analytics

## Business Problem
Hospital administrators face critical challenges in managing patient flow, bed utilisation, and resource allocation across departments and regions. This project analyses 318,438 patient admissions to identify operational inefficiencies and provide data-driven recommendations.

## Key Findings
1. **Gynecology handles 78.3% of all admissions** — extreme departmental concentration requiring urgent staffing rebalancing
2. **Visitor count is the strongest predictor of stay length** (r = 0.541) — stronger than illness severity or bed grade
3. **Bed Grade 4 wards show longest average stays** (33.4 days) — infrastructure investment would directly reduce bed-blocking
4. **Region X carries 41.9% of total patient load** — regional capacity distribution is significantly imbalanced
5. **Minor illness cases cost more than Extreme cases** (Rs.4,982 vs Rs.4,748) — pricing policy anomaly identified

## Dataset
- **Source:** AV Healthcare Analytics II (Kaggle)
- **Size:** 318,438 patient admissions | 18 variables
- **Coverage:** 3 hospital regions, 5 departments, multiple ward types

## Business Recommendations
1. Redistribute patient load from Gynecology through triage protocol revision
2. Implement family liaison programme for high-visitor patients
3. Prioritise Bed Grade 4 ward upgrades in Region X
4. Review admission deposit structure for Minor illness cases
5. Commission regional capacity audit for Regions Y and Z
